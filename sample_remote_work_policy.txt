REMOTE WORK POLICY 2025

Document Version: 1.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Department: Human Resources

PURPOSE
This policy outlines the guidelines and expectations for employees who work remotely, either full-time or on a hybrid schedule, to ensure productivity, security, and work-life balance.

1. ELIGIBILITY

1.1 Eligible Positions
Not all positions are suitable for remote work. Eligibility is determined by:
- Nature of job duties (can be performed remotely without loss of productivity)
- Employee performance history (must have satisfactory performance record)
- Minimum tenure: 6 months with the company
- Manager approval required

1.2 Ineligible Positions
Positions requiring physical presence:
- Reception and front desk
- On-site IT support
- Facilities management
- Positions handling physical inventory
- Lab technicians

2. WORK ARRANGEMENTS

2.1 Full Remote
- Work from home 5 days per week
- Must attend in-person meetings when required (with 1 week notice)
- Quarterly in-office team meetings

2.2 Hybrid Schedule
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval.

Office Days:
- Minimum 2 days in office per week
- Core days: Tuesdays and Thursdays (highly recommended for collaboration)
- Schedule must be submitted and approved monthly

2.3 Occasional Remote Work
Employees may request ad-hoc remote work days:
- Maximum 2 days per month
- Manager approval required with 24 hours notice
- Subject to business needs

3. WORKING HOURS

3.1 Core Hours
All remote employees must be available during core business hours:
- Core Hours: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM (Local Time)
- Flexible start time: Between 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM
- Flexible end time: Between 3:00 PM - 7:00 PM

3.2 Availability
- Must be reachable via email, Slack, and video calls during working hours
- Response time expectation: Within 2 hours during core hours
- Set status indicators (Available, In Meeting, Away, Offline)

3.3 Breaks and Lunch
- 30-minute lunch break (unpaid)
- 15-minute breaks (morning and afternoon)
- Update calendar to show break times

4. WORKSPACE REQUIREMENTS

4.1 Home Office Setup
Employees must have:
- Dedicated, quiet workspace
- Stable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Adequate lighting
- Ergonomic furniture (desk and chair)

4.2 Equipment Provided by Company
- Laptop/Desktop computer
- Monitor (upon request)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset with microphone
- Docking station (if applicable)

4.3 Employee Responsibility
- Power supply and electrical costs
- Internet service provider costs
- Office furniture (company may provide allowance up to $500)
- Stationery and office supplies

5. EQUIPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY

5.1 Company Equipment
- All company-provided equipment remains company property
- Must be returned upon termination or end of remote work arrangement
- Report any damage or malfunction immediately to IT

5.2 Security Requirements
- Use company VPN for all work-related activities
- Keep software and operating systems updated
- Use encrypted connections for sensitive data
- Never share passwords or login credentials
- Lock computer when stepping away

5.3 Technical Support
- IT helpdesk available 8 AM - 6 PM EST
- Emergency support available 24/7
- Remote troubleshooting via TeamViewer
- On-site support available by appointment

6. COMMUNICATION AND COLLABORATION

6.1 Required Tools
- Email: Primary communication tool
- Slack: Instant messaging and team collaboration
- Zoom: Video conferencing
- Microsoft Teams: File sharing and collaboration
- Jira/Asana: Project management

6.2 Meeting Etiquette
- Video on for team meetings
- Mute when not speaking
- Use virtual backgrounds if workspace not professional
- Test audio/video before meetings
- Be punctual

6.3 Daily Check-ins
- Morning: Brief status update in team Slack channel
- End of day: Summary of completed tasks
- Weekly: 1:1 with manager

7. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS

7.1 Productivity
Remote work is a privilege based on sustained performance:
- Meet all deadlines and deliverables
- Maintain quality standards
- Respond promptly to communications
- Participate actively in meetings

7.2 Performance Monitoring
- Regular 1:1 meetings with manager
- Weekly progress reports
- Project milestone tracking
- Quarterly performance reviews

7.3 Termination of Remote Work Privilege
Remote work arrangement may be revoked if:
- Performance declines
- Communication issues arise
- Security breaches occur
- Business needs change

8. DATA SECURITY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Data Protection
- No storing of company data on personal devices
- Use company cloud storage (OneDrive, SharePoint)
- Encrypt all sensitive documents
- Shred physical documents properly

8.2 Privacy
- Ensure confidential conversations cannot be overheard
- Use headphones for sensitive calls
- Never discuss confidential matters in public spaces
- Lock doors during sensitive meetings

8.3 Cybersecurity
- Report any suspicious emails or activities immediately
- Never use public Wi-Fi for work without VPN
- Keep antivirus software updated
- Back up work regularly

9. EXPENSES AND REIMBURSEMENTS

9.1 Internet Stipend
- $50/month internet reimbursement for full remote employees
- $25/month for hybrid employees
- Submit receipts monthly via expense system

9.2 Home Office Setup Allowance
One-time allowance of $500 for:
- Desk
- Ergonomic chair
- Lighting
- Other office essentials

Submit itemized receipts within first 90 days of remote work approval.

9.3 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- Electricity
- Heating/Cooling
- Home insurance
- Property taxes

10. HEALTH AND SAFETY

10.1 Ergonomics
- Follow ergonomic guidelines provided in company handbook
- Take regular breaks to avoid strain
- Adjust chair and monitor height properly

10.2 Work-Life Balance
- Maintain clear boundaries between work and personal time
- Avoid working excessive hours
- Take full lunch breaks away from computer
- Use vacation time

10.3 Reporting Injuries
Report any work-related injuries to HR within 24 hours, even if working from home.

11. IN-PERSON REQUIREMENTS

11.1 Mandatory Office Attendance
Remote employees must attend in-person for:
- Quarterly all-hands meetings
- Department offsites and team building events
- Critical project kickoffs
- Performance reviews (as needed)
- Client meetings (as requested)

11.2 Travel Expenses
Company will cover travel expenses for mandatory in-person events:
- Transportation costs (flights, gas mileage, parking)
- Accommodation (if over 50 miles from office)
- Per diem for meals

12. INTERNATIONAL REMOTE WORK

12.1 Within Country
Employees may work remotely from any location within their country of residence, subject to:
- Tax implications
- Legal requirements
- Time zone considerations (must overlap with core hours)

12.2 International
International remote work requires special approval from:
- HR Department
- Legal Department
- Tax/Finance Department

Considerations:
- Visa and work permit requirements
- Tax treaties and obligations
- Time zone differences
- Equipment shipping

13. POLICY COMPLIANCE

13.1 Acknowledgment
All remote employees must sign a Remote Work Agreement acknowledging they have read and understood this policy.

13.2 Violations
Policy violations may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Termination of remote work privilege
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination

14. POLICY REVIEW

This policy is reviewed annually and may be updated based on:
- Business needs
- Technology changes
- Legal requirements
- Employee feedback

15. QUESTIONS AND SUPPORT

For questions about this policy, contact:
- HR Department: hr@company.com
- IT Support: itsupport@company.com
- Remote Work Coordinator: remotework@company.com
